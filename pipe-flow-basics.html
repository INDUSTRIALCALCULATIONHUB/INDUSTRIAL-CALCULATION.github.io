Pipe Flow Basics Explained

Understanding fluid flow in pipelines and industrial systems

🔧 What is Pipe Flow?

Pipe flow refers to the movement of fluids (liquids or gases) through a closed conduit. It is widely used in water supply, oil pipelines, chemical plants, and industrial systems.

📐 Key Pipe Flow Concepts

  • Flow rate (Q)
  • Velocity of fluid
  • Pressure drop
  • Pipe diameter
  • Friction losses

⚙️ Governing Equation

Pipe flow is mainly governed by Bernoulli’s equation and continuity equation. Friction losses are calculated using Darcy-Weisbach equation.

📉 Pressure Loss in Pipes

As fluid flows through a pipe, energy is lost due to friction between fluid and pipe walls. Longer pipes and smaller diameters increase pressure loss.

🏭 Applications

  • Water distribution systems
  • Oil and gas pipelines
  • HVAC systems
  • Chemical processing plants
