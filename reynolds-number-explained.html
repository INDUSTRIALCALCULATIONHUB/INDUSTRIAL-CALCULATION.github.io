What is Reynolds Number?

Understanding Laminar and Turbulent Flow in Fluid Mechanics

📌 Introduction

The Reynolds number is a fundamental concept in fluid mechanics used to predict the flow pattern of a fluid inside a pipe or around an object. It helps engineers determine whether the flow is laminar or turbulent.

📊 Reynolds Number Formula

The Reynolds number is defined as:

Re = ρ × V × D / μ

  • ρ = Fluid density (kg/m³)
  • V = Velocity of fluid (m/s)
  • D = Pipe diameter (m)
  • μ = Dynamic viscosity (Pa·s)

🌊 Flow Types

1. Laminar Flow

Occurs when Re < 2000. Fluid moves in smooth layers with no mixing.

2. Transitional Flow

Occurs when Re is between 2000 and 4000. Flow is unstable.

3. Turbulent Flow

Occurs when Re > 4000. Fluid motion is chaotic and mixed.

🏭 Engineering Applications

  • Pipe flow design
  • Oil and gas pipelines
  • Chemical process industries
  • Water distribution systems
