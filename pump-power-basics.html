⚙️ Pump Motor Selection Basics

Understanding hydraulic power and pump energy requirements

🔧 What is Pump Power?

Pump power is the energy required to move fluid against gravity, friction, and system resistance. It is a fundamental concept in fluid mechanics and hydraulic engineering used for pump selection.

📐 Pump Power Formula

P = (ρ × g × Q × H) / η
  • ρ = Fluid density (kg/m³)
  • g = 9.81 m/s²
  • Q = Flow rate (m³/s)
  • H = Head (m)
  • η = Efficiency

⚙️ Working Principle

A pump increases the energy of a fluid by converting mechanical energy into hydraulic energy. Higher flow rate or head requires higher motor power.

🏭 Industrial Applications

  • Water supply systems
  • Oil & gas pipelines
  • Cooling systems
  • Irrigation systems

📊 Engineering Importance

Correct pump motor selection ensures energy efficiency, reduces operating cost, and improves system reliability. Oversized or undersized pumps lead to performance issues.

📘 Theory / Information / Notes

Pump selection depends on system head, flow rate, fluid density, and efficiency. Proper hydraulic design ensures stable operation and prevents cavitation and energy losses in pipelines.
