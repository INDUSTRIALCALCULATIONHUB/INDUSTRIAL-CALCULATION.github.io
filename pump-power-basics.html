Pump Power Basics Explained

Understand hydraulic pump power in simple engineering terms

🔧 What is Pump Power?

Pump power is the energy required to move a fluid from one location to another against gravity, friction, and system resistance. It is a key concept in fluid mechanics and hydraulic engineering.

📐 Pump Power Formula

The standard formula used in engineering is:

P = (ρ × g × Q × H) / η

  • ρ = Fluid density (kg/m³)
  • g = Gravity (9.81 m/s²)
  • Q = Flow rate (m³/s)
  • H = Head (m)
  • η = Efficiency

⚙️ How Pump Power Works

A pump increases the energy of a fluid by increasing its pressure and velocity. The higher the flow rate or head, the more power is required.

🏭 Real-World Applications

  • Water supply systems
  • Oil and gas pipelines
  • Cooling systems in industries
  • Irrigation and agriculture pumps
